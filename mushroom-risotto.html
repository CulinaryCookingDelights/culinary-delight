Creamy Mushroom Risotto

A luxurious Italian rice dish featuring a medley of mushrooms, rich Parmesan, and white wine, slowly cooked to creamy perfection.

Main Course Italian Vegetarian
Prep: 15 mins
Cook: 30 mins
Serves: 4
Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups Arborio rice
  • 8 cups mushroom or vegetable stock, kept warm
  • 1 pound mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, porcini), sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: truffle oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the stock and keep it warm throughout the cooking process
  2. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon each of butter and olive oil. Sauté mushrooms until golden brown. Set aside
  3. In the same pan, heat remaining oil and butter. Add onions and cook until translucent
  4. Add garlic and rice, stirring to coat the rice with oil until lightly toasted
  5. Pour in the wine and stir until almost completely absorbed
  6. Begin adding warm stock, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly until each addition is absorbed
  7. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes, until rice is creamy and al dente
  8. Stir in the sautéed mushrooms, Parmesan cheese, and thyme
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste
  10. Let rest for 2 minutes, then serve with additional Parmesan and optional truffle oil

Chef's Tips

  • Use room temperature stock to maintain consistent cooking temperature
  • Never wash mushrooms - brush them clean instead to preserve their flavor
  • Keep stirring! This releases the rice's starches and creates the signature creamy texture
  • The risotto should be loose and creamy, not stiff - add extra hot stock if needed
  • For extra richness, try these variations:
  • Add a splash of heavy cream at the end
  • Mix in some mascarpone cheese
  • Top with crispy fried sage leaves
