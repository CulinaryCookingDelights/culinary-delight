Classic Shrimp Cocktail

Perfectly poached shrimp served with a zesty homemade cocktail sauce. This elegant appetizer is perfect for special occasions and holiday gatherings.

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 10 mins
Servings: 8
Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

For the Shrimp:

  • 2 pounds large shrimp (16-20 count), peeled and deveined
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • Additional lemon wedges for serving

For the Cocktail Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 3 tablespoons prepared horseradish
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon hot sauce (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Poaching Liquid

    Fill a large pot with water. Add halved lemon, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt. Bring to a boil over high heat.

  2. Cook the Shrimp

    Add the shrimp to the boiling water. Cook until they turn pink and curl into a loose "C" shape, about 3-4 minutes. Immediately transfer to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.

  3. Make the Cocktail Sauce

    In a medium bowl, combine ketchup, horseradish, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, and hot sauce (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

  4. Chill and Serve

    Drain the shrimp and pat dry. Refrigerate until completely chilled, at least 1 hour. Serve with the cocktail sauce and lemon wedges.

Chef's Tips

  • For the best texture, avoid overcooking the shrimp. They should be just pink and opaque.
  • You can prepare the cocktail sauce up to 3 days in advance.
  • Leave the tails on for an elegant presentation and easier handling.
  • Adjust the amount of horseradish in the cocktail sauce to your preferred level of heat.
