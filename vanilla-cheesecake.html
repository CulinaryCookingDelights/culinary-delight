Classic Vanilla Cheesecake

A rich and creamy New York-style cheesecake with a buttery graham cracker crust. This classic dessert is perfect for special occasions or whenever you're craving something truly indulgent.

Prep Time: 30 mins
Cook Time: 45 mins
Servings: 12
Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the Filling:

  • 4 (8 oz) packages cream cheese, softened
  • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1/3 cup heavy cream
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For Serving (Optional):

  • Fresh berries
  • Whipped cream
  • Caramel sauce

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pan

    Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Wrap the outside of a 9-inch springform pan with aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in during baking.

  2. Make the Crust

    In a medium bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, sugar, and salt. Press mixture firmly into the bottom and about 1 inch up the sides of the prepared pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let cool.

  3. Prepare the Filling

    In a large bowl, beat cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add sugar and beat until creamy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in heavy cream, vanilla extract, and salt until just combined.

  4. Bake the Cheesecake

    Pour filling into the cooled crust. Place the springform pan in a larger roasting pan and fill with hot water halfway up the sides of the springform pan. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle.

  5. Cool and Chill

    Turn off the oven and crack the door open. Let the cheesecake cool in the oven for 1 hour. Remove from the water bath, run a knife around the edge, and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  6. Serve

    Remove from springform pan and serve with optional toppings of your choice.

Chef's Tips

  • Ensure all ingredients are at room temperature before starting.
  • Don't overmix the filling after adding the eggs to prevent cracks.
  • The water bath helps prevent cracks and ensures even baking.
  • Store in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
  • Can be frozen for up to 2 months when wrapped well.
