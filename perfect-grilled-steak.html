Perfect Grilled Steak

Master the art of grilling the perfect steak with this foolproof method. Achieve restaurant-quality results with a beautiful crust and juicy, tender center.

Main Course Beef Grilling
Prep: 30 mins
Cook: 12 mins
Serves: 2
Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1½-inch thick)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons coarse sea salt
  • 2 tablespoons freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Optional: compound butter for serving

Instructions

  1. Remove steaks from refrigerator 30 minutes before cooking to bring to room temperature
  2. Pat steaks dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture
  3. Season generously with salt and pepper on all sides
  4. Preheat your grill to high heat (450-500°F)
  5. Clean and oil the grill grates
  6. Place steaks on the hot grill. For medium-rare, cook for:
    • 5 minutes on first side
    • 4 minutes on second side
  7. Add butter, crushed garlic, and rosemary in the last minute of cooking
  8. Remove from grill and let rest for 5-10 minutes before serving
  9. Slice against the grain and serve with compound butter if desired

Chef's Tips

  • Choose high-quality steaks with good marbling for the best results.
  • Internal temperature guide:
    • Rare: 125°F
    • Medium-rare: 135°F
    • Medium: 145°F
  • Always use tongs to flip the steak, never a fork which can pierce the meat.
  • The resting period is crucial - it allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.
  • For cross-hatch grill marks, rotate the steak 45 degrees halfway through cooking on each side.
